## How We Ranked These Creators

Before diving into the list, it's worth understanding what separates a KolBuff ranking from a simple follower count leaderboard. Our Heat Score (0–100) weighs four key signals:

- **Engagement Rate** — Likes, comments, shares, and saves relative to follower count. A creator with 500K followers and a 6% engagement rate outranks one with 2M followers and 0.8%.
- **Growth Velocity** — Month-over-month follower and view growth. Are they trending up or plateauing?
- **Content Consistency** — Posting frequency and quality over the last 90 days. One viral hit doesn't make a top creator.
- **Audience Authenticity** — Our bot detection and engagement pattern analysis filters out creators with suspicious follower spikes or engagement farming.

The result is a score that reflects *real influence*, not vanity metrics.

## Engagement vs. Followers: The Malaysian Paradox

One of the most interesting findings in our data is what we call the "Malaysian Engagement Paradox": creators in the 500K–2M follower range consistently outperform mega-creators (5M+) on engagement rate.

The numbers tell the story:

- **Under 500K followers:** Average engagement rate of 6.2%
- **500K–2M followers:** Average engagement rate of 7.4%
- **2M–5M followers:** Average engagement rate of 4.1%
- **Over 5M followers:** Average engagement rate of 2.8%

This sweet spot exists because mid-tier Malaysian creators tend to maintain stronger community connections — they respond to comments in Bahasa Malaysia, create content about local issues, and haven't yet been diluted by international audience growth.

For brands, this has a clear implication: **a campaign with three 800K-follower creators will likely outperform a single 5M-follower creator** on actual engagement and conversion, often at lower total cost.
